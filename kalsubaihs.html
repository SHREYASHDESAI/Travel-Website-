Kalsubai (Marathi: कळसूबाई शिखर) is a mountain in the Western Ghats, located in the Indian state of Maharashtra. Kalsubai is the highest peak in Maharashtra and is located in Ahmednagar district in Akole Taluka. Its summit, situated at an elevation of 1,646 metres (5,400 ft), is the highest point in Maharashtra. Hence, known as the Everest of Maharashtra. The mountain range lies within the Kalsubai-Harishchandragad Wildlife Sanctuary. It is visited throughout the year by avid trekkers, Kalsubai temple devotees and wildlife enthusiasts. It is named after one of the three sisters Kalsubai, Ratnabai and Katrabai. The other peak Ratangad is named after Ratnabai.

Major Attraction:

  1. Bhandardara Dam - Serene reservoir offering boating and scenic views.
  2. Arthur Lake - Picturesque lake surrounded by lush hills
  3. Ratangad Fort - Historic hill fort with ancient temples and caves
  4. Sandhan Valley - Adventure destination known for rappelling and rock climbing
  5. Amruteshwar Temple - Ancient Hindu temple with intricate architecture
  6. Wilson Dam - Oldest dam in India with stunning valley views
  7. Umbrella Falls - Seasonal waterfall resembling an umbrella, picturesque during monsoon
